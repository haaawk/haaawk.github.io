--- layout: post title: "ScyllaDB/Cassandra SSTable format" subtitle: "Variant integers encoding" date: 2018-02-26 10:30:25 +0100 ---

SSTable format uses a concept of variant integers borrowed from [Protocol Buffers Bas 128 Variants] (https://developers.google.com/protocol-buffers/docs/encoding#varints). Encoding isn't exactly the same but it shares the idea that a number does not have to be represented by a fixed number of bytes. In SSTables, a variant integer can be represented by 1-9 bytes. The length of the representation is encoded in the first byte.
